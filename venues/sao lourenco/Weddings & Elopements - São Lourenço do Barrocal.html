Inspiration

Welcome to São Lourenço do Barrocal, a rural retreat sensitive to the vastness of its surrounding lands and the family legacy that lies at its origins, 200-years-ago, with an ancient small farming village at its heart. This traditional “monte alentejano” was carefully brought back to life as a remarkable hotel of understated luxury, located among vineyards, oaks and centuries-old olive groves making up the landscape of Monsaraz, Portugal.

In this truly extraordinary landscape you can enjoy an immense blue sky that provides such a warm welcome to all who enter it. Feel embraced by the monumental granite rocks that decided to make this their everlasting home. All of this makes São Lourenço do Barrocal the perfect place to gather friends and family to celebrate life together.

Location

The Alentejo is one of the best preserved landscapes and one of the least populated regions of southern Europe. Its vast open country is home both to tradition and contemporary culture, renewing itself as a region that is global but not globalised. The sense of beauty is found almost everywhere, from whitewashed villages and craggy outcrops to undulating plains peppered with cork oak and olive trees. Fairly close to Lisbon, it occupies almost one-third of Portugal. Yet, somehow, it is still a confined region, inhabited by friendly people and a quiet charm.

© Arte Magna
© Enrico Constantini

São Lourenço do Barrocal is located in Alentejo’s eastern side, just next door to the stunning hill-top medieval village of Monsaraz, the “wine capital” of Reguengos de Monsaraz and the Alqueva Lake. A wine region by excellence, this area is also known for it’s megalithic heritage, ceramic works (mostly because of the São Pedro do Corval village, the biggest pottery hub in Portugal), water sports, sport fishing and also for being the world’s first Dark Sky Reserve, because of it’s natural disposition for the observation of stars and other cosmic wonders.

Lisbon is a two hour drive away from Barrocal but half-way between these tho places you’ll find Évora, one of Portugal’s most charming, centuries-old town. Shaped by more than 2000 years of history, this UNESCO certified world heritage city is a jewel waiting to be discovered.

Venues

Settings rich in history, agricultural memories and architectural beauty, open air terraces with views over the fields, olive groves, orchards and green meadows with wild flowers, all lay more than 1500 sq. m. at your disposal and of your guests, with capacity for up to 120 persons.

MENHIR

Inside the estate there is one of the most significant megalithic monuments of the Iberian Peninsula, a 7,000-year-old menhir that rises amongst holm-oaks and centuries-old olive trees. With a clear view of the fairytale-like castle of Monsaraz, this place is perfect for a celebration full of symbolism or a picnic with privileged views.

OLD APIARY

In the circular space where beehives used to be set up there’s now an amazing space, surrounded by a wall full of arches and ancient symbols left by previous generations. This is a multifaceted setting, perfect to host either the celebration itself or your guests while they dance the night away.

ANCIENT BULLRING

Formerly dedicated to the ancient Iberian tradition of bullfighting, this space still functions today as an open air arena, but dedicated to festivities that celebrate some of the most special occasions in life. A great location to serve an original cocktail, either before or after the ceremony, to host a relaxed al fresco dinner or even throw a party with live performers.

SHEEP PEN

In the old sheep pen, where the Merino wool sheep were gathered, it is possible to find a walled labyrinth-like garden lined with ivies and a green meadow. A very iconic and special place, ideal for serving cocktails or casual meals for small gatherings.

HORTELÃO AND ORGANIC GARDEN

At the barbecue-inspired Hortelão restaurant guests can have a taste of our freshly grilled vegetables, our delicious organic veal, Alentejo acorn pork and even fish from Lake Alqueva. Nestled between the swimming pool and the organic vegetable garden, this is perfect for dining under the starry sky of the Alentejo, on warm summer nights. Can be used as a more relaxed setting for welcome dinners or for your al-fresco wedding dinner.

POOL BAR

This relaxed setting overlooking the pool and the unexpected beauty of an organic vegetable garden is also at your disposal. Here, drinks can be served as appetizers to your welcome dinner but breakfasts can also take place here or even light meals for small gatherings.

RESTAURANT

Step inside our main restaurant to find a beautiful main room with a fireplace and a cabinet of curiosities and also a small private room. Outside, two terraces under shade-providing pergolas offer views out to the countryside and life on the estate. On the meadow side, a well from the old farm’s irrigation system has been converted into an outdoor firepit, a cosy spot for nightcaps. Food here is from Alentejo of course.

WINE TOURISM TERRACE

Winemaking is in the Estate’s DNA and that makes the wine tourism area one of its main locations. Right next to the winery you’ll find a shaded terrace that connects to a long lawn that is perfect for your wedding dinner or even as a cocktail area. With a beautiful view of the surrounding natural landscape, it’s one of the best places in the Estate to watch a summery sunset.

HOTEL BAR

Our bar is available to serve some delicious drinks – including our cocktails and estate-produced wines – as well as finger food specialties. This can happen in the arched area of the bar or in the adjacent room, that is more spacious and has a stunning outside area that is perfect for a beautiful sunset drink.

ARCHED GALLERY

An architectural setting in itself: a covered gallery (that once served as parking space for the donkey carts and horse carriages) beautifuly framed by arches with hanging green ivy, and opening up to the main cobblestone street of the old ‘monte’; this is a space destined for long running tables, able to enjoy the outdoors even under the protection of a roof.

Food

The freshest fruits and vegetables come from our orchard and organic garden to your plate, so as the most characteristic herbs of the region’s gastronomy. Honouring and showcasing the best the local tradition has to offer but always with our feet firmly on our contemporary world, these delicacies include homegrown ingredients and products from the estate, but also other products delivered by small producers from around the Alentejo region.

download menus

CERTIFIED ORGANIC PRODUCTION

Respect for nature, responsible farming and conscious interpretation of the region’s immense farming culture are key aspects of São Lourenço do Barrocal and are present in many aspects of the project: one of the more relevant ones is the organic approach to farming. All of the estate is certified in this way in order to make sure that what you eat and drink is not just as flavourful as it can be but also as less impactful as possible.

Our certified products: cattle, olives, olive oil, wine, vegetables, fruits, herbs, honey, compotes.

Examples of outsourced ingredients: Regional goat and sheep cheeses, fish from Alqueva lake, Iberian acorn-fed pig, artisanal bread from the nearby village of Baldio, gin and “medronho” firewater.

SELF-SUSTAINABLE CYCLE

We are proud to say that all of the production cycle of our products are enclosed in the estate, from feeding to reproduction in animals, from seed to plate in plants, from grapes to wine and from olives to olive oil. It’s also how we honour the legacy of the families that used to live in Barrocal, throughout it’s 200 years of history, that lived and produced most of what they needed.

SEASONAL INGREDIENTS

Seasonality is one of the biggest - and most obvious - guidelines for all the things you will taste in São Lourenço do Barrocal, that’s why we not only change our menus very often but sometimes are forced to do minor changes in already established dishes to better respect the specific seasons of each ingredient.

FROM ALENTEJO TO THE WORLD: A TAYLOR MADE EXPERIENCE

Alentejo regional cuisine is our backbone and big part of our DNA, that’s is why we always choose to highlight its most delicious and important elements in all our menus. FAQ

If you have a specific enquiry or would like to speak further with us, please get in touch through our e-mail: info@barrocal.pt.

Can you give me directions on how to reach São Lourenço do Barrocal? The estate is located next to Monsaraz, a small village in the interior of the Alentejo region of Portugal. The best way to reach us, when coming from abroad, would be by flying to either the Lisbon or the Faro (south of the country, in the Algarve region) international airports. When you arrive, in both cases you'll have a tranquil two-hour drive until you reach Barrocal. In case you don't want to drive yourself with a rental car, transfer services can be provided by the hotel, via an outside partner. There is also the possibility of arriving by small plane or private jet via the Évora airfield or the Beja Airport, which are about 45 minutes away from the estate.

Are there other accommodation alternatives for my guests who are not staying at the estate? Yes, there are. Even though Barrocal exists in a rural area, where nature and wildlife thrive, there are several options, with different price ranges, available for your choosing. Our team will happily help you by suggesting the type of accommodation that best fits your needs.

How about wedding planners? São Lourenço do Barrocal doesn't have its own wedding planner but our past experience in working with these professionals has given us a great list of wonderful national and international references, in case you haven't chosen yours. We truly believe that working with a wedding planner that is perfectly aligned with your preferences and expectations is key to having a magical ceremony, that is why we do our best in trying to help you find the perfect match.

I would like to have my ceremony presided over by a religious and/or legal entity. What are my options? São Lourenço do Barrocal doesn't have any type of religious infrastructure or dedicated prayer room. Despite that, we can help you find solutions for both of these types of celebrations in our immediate surroundings.

What could we do during our time at Barrocal in the days leading up to and right after the ceremony? Alentejo culture has always praised the importance of taking things slow, of contemplating nature and making the most out of simple pleasures like a great glass of wine or an excellent meal. At the estate, you'll find all of this, of course, but there is also a big selection of activities that can add a little something extra to your experience. Always deeply connected with the region's culture and biodiversity, as well as the estate's traditions and history, this list includes stargazing, wine tastings, birding sessions, among many others, for both kids and grown-ups. Feel free to ask us for more details about these activities and how you can include them in your celebration.

What type of food can we expect to find at the hotel and what are my options when choosing how to cater my event? Barrocal's 200-year-old legacy has always had a deep connection with farming - be it through fruits and vegetables, olives and olive oil, grapes and wine, meat, grains, honey, and more. For this reason, a sustainable and season-conscious approach to food is a given. We do our best to use as much as we can of what we produce ourselves and work closely with regional and national suppliers to serve food that is deeply connected with Alentejo's traditional cooking but always through a more contemporary outlook. Tradition and elegance walk hand-in-hand with a sustainable approach to the sourcing of ingredients. We are of course happy and available to tailormade our offer to perfectly fit in specific requests about allergies, restrictions, preferences, or personal favourites.

How many guests can I welcome at my wedding? As one would expect of a hotel that is also a fully functioning farm, outdoor spaces are a major highlight. There are plenty to choose from, all of them crossing the best that nature offers with the comforts of contemporary life, and where large groups can celebrate. Depending on what you wish to organize, our team can adjust the settings according to your preferences. If you're looking for a more intimate, indoor gathering, our venues can host up to a maximum of 120 people.

Are there any specific curfews or noise limits? The Alentejo is Portugal's biggest region in terms of territory and one of the least populated areas in Europe, thus making it a place where absolute tranquility and privacy are easily obtained. Barrocal is a great example of this, especially because it is located inside an estate with almost 800 hectares and with basically no neighbours at all. Because of this - and also of the fact that exclusivity is mandatory when hosting a wedding - the party can last as long as you please, without limitations.

Weather-wise, what can we expect from this region? In general, Portugal is blessed with mostly sunny and warm weather almost year-round. Alentejo, more specifically, is prone to this type of weather, with an average annual temperature of 23º Celsius (73ºF) and an approximate maximum of eight full days of rain per year. Between April and September, the average daily temperature rises a bit above 25º Celsius (77ºF), with the months of June, July and August being the warmest. Spring and Autumn are the times of the year when the weather is the most balanced, with nice and not-overly warm sunny days. In the wintertime, grayer skies, chillier winds, and the occasional drizzle are to be expected. But don’t worry: if need, there is always the option of setting up an events tent, depending on location and size.

Do I have to have the hotel in exclusivity, when celebrating my wedding at Barrocal? Yes. In order to guarantee that our entire team is fully focused on your special days and that the stay of other guests who are not part of the celebration is safeguarded, weddings have a mandatory buyout of two or three days.

But what about elopements? Do they have to follow the same rule? No, they don't. Elopements don't require a buyout as long as they don't include more than 20 guests. Feel free to discuss this topic further with our team.
